9 Most Devestating Earthquakes of All Time

Isabella Ogorzalek

Earthquake Name Date and Location Magnitude Epicenter Depth Summary of Damage and Number of Fatalities
Shaanxi earthquake China, Jan 23, 1556 8 20 km Considered the deadliest earthquake in history, killing 60% of the regions population with 830,000 deaths total.
Tangshan earthquake China, July 28, 1976 7.8 15 km Destroyed over 180 buildings within 10 seconds, resulted in 800,000 injuries and 255,000 deaths overall.
Sumatra earthquake Indonesia, Dec. 26, 2004 9.1 80 km Fourteen countries off the coast of Indian Ocean could feel affects from this earthquake, resulting in 227,000 deaths, 50,000 individuals gone missing, and 1.7 million were displaced.
Haiti earthquake Haiti, Jan. 12, 2010 7 13 km Immense damage was caused to houses and building structures throughout the area, with over 10,000 houses destroyed, and 300,000 injuries.
Damghan earthquake Iran, Dec. 22, 856 7.9 16.5 km More than 200,000 people were killed, resulting in the highest casualty count in Iranian history.
Haiyuan earthquake China, Dec. 16, 1920 8.5 15km Took more than 200,000 lives, and affected other countries nearby due to its magnitude.
Ardabil earthquake Iran, on Mar. 23, 893 6.1 10 km Affected many individuals, taking more than 150,000 lives.
Kanto earthquake Japan, Sept. 1, 1923 7.9 20 km Deadliest earthquake in Japan's history, with 380 houses burned afterwards, and resulted in 142,000 deaths.
Ashgabat earthquake Turkmenistan, Oct. 5, 1948 7.3 48 km Taking place in this countries capital, it killed over 110,000 people.
